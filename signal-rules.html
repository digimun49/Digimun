📝 Core Rules

Essential Signal Rules

Follow these rules strictly to improve your trading accuracy and protect your investment.

1

Avoid Gap Up / Gap Down

If you see a clear gap on the chart (price jumps without trading in between), skip that trade completely. Gaps indicate high volatility and unpredictable price action.

2

Skip Doji Candles

If the previous candle is a Doji (open and close are nearly equal), avoid the trade. Doji candles signal market indecision and increase the risk of loss significantly.

3

Watch the 78% Level

If the market percentage shows 78%, refrain from trading. This level is often prone to broker manipulation and unexpected reversals.

4

Enter at Safety Margin

Always wait for a good entry point with a safety margin. Never rush into trades - patience is key to consistent profits in binary options.

5

Use 1-Step Martingale

If a trade results in a loss, apply the 1-Step Martingale strategy on the next signal. This helps recover losses while managing risk appropriately.

6

Proper Money Management

Never risk more than 2-5% of your total capital on a single trade. Consistent money management is the foundation of long-term trading success.

7

Watch Candle Wick Length

If the previous candle's wick is longer than its body, be extra cautious. Either skip the trade or enter with the highest safety margin.

8

MTG Loss Protocol

If 1 signal loses with MTG, and the second signal from the same pair also loses directly - do NOT take MTG. If second signal from another pair loses MTG too, skip ALL future signals for that session.

The Martingale Strategy

A recovery system to recoup losses - use with discipline and strict limits

1

Base Trade

Start with your standard trade amount (e.g., $1)

2

If Loss

Double your trade amount on next signal ($2)

3

If Loss Again

Double again only if different pair ($4)

4

Stop Loss

2 consecutive MTG losses = STOP trading

⚠️

Important MTG Warning

Martingale can be risky if not controlled. Always set a maximum of 1-2 steps. Never chase losses beyond your predetermined limit. If you hit 2 consecutive MTG losses, stop trading for that session and return with a fresh mindset.
