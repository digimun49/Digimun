Bollinger Bands Strategy for Binary Options

Bollinger Bands consist of a middle band (SMA) with upper and lower bands set at standard deviations away. They measure market volatility and identify potential price extremes.

How Bollinger Bands Works

The bands expand during high volatility and contract during low volatility. Price touching the upper band suggests overbought conditions, while touching the lower band suggests oversold conditions. Band squeezes precede breakouts.

Recommended Settings

Period: 20, Standard Deviation: 2

Best Use Case

Volatility-based trading and mean reversion strategies. Bollinger Bands is particularly effective when combined with other indicators and price action analysis for confirmation.

Bollinger Bands Trading Strategy

For mean reversion: enter CALL when price touches the lower band with RSI below 30. Enter PUT when price touches the upper band with RSI above 70. In strong trends, use band riding strategy.

Tips for Using Bollinger Bands in Binary Options

  • Combine with trend analysis — Always confirm Bollinger Bands signals with the overall market trend
  • Use multiple timeframes — Check the signal on both 1-minute and 5-minute charts
  • Wait for confirmation — Don't enter on the first signal; wait for candlestick confirmation
  • Practice on demo first — Test your Bollinger Bands strategy on a demo account before trading real money
  • Apply money management — Never risk more than 1-3% of your account per trade
