RSI (Relative Strength Index) Strategy for Binary Options

The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a momentum oscillator that measures the speed and change of price movements on a scale of 0-100. It identifies overbought and oversold conditions.

How RSI Works

RSI calculates the ratio of upward price movements to downward movements over a specified period (typically 14). Values above 70 indicate overbought conditions (potential PUT signal), while values below 30 indicate oversold conditions (potential CALL signal).

Recommended Settings

Period: 14, Overbought: 70, Oversold: 30

Best Use Case

Identifying reversal points and confirming trend strength. RSI is particularly effective when combined with other indicators and price action analysis for confirmation.

RSI Trading Strategy

Wait for RSI to cross above 30 from below for CALL entries, or cross below 70 from above for PUT entries. Combine with trend direction for higher accuracy.

Tips for Using RSI in Binary Options

  • Combine with trend analysis — Always confirm RSI signals with the overall market trend
  • Use multiple timeframes — Check the signal on both 1-minute and 5-minute charts
  • Wait for confirmation — Don't enter on the first signal; wait for candlestick confirmation
  • Practice on demo first — Test your RSI strategy on a demo account before trading real money
  • Apply money management — Never risk more than 1-3% of your account per trade
