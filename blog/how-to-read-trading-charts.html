How to Read Trading Charts: Complete Beginner's Guide

February 10, 20267 min readBy Digimun Pro Team

If you've ever looked at a trading chart and felt overwhelmed by all the colors, lines, and patterns, this guide is for you. By the end of this article, you'll be able to read any trading chart confidently and understand what the market is telling you.

Understanding Chart Types

Candlestick Charts (Most Important)

Candlestick charts are the gold standard for binary options trading. Each candle represents one time period (1 minute, 5 minutes, etc.) and shows four pieces of information:

  • Open — The price when the candle started
  • Close — The price when the candle ended
  • High — The highest price during the period
  • Low — The lowest price during the period

Green (or white) candles mean the close was higher than the open — price went UP. Red (or black) candles mean the close was lower than the open — price went DOWN.

Line Charts

Line charts connect closing prices with a simple line. They're useful for seeing the overall trend direction at a glance but lack the detailed information candlesticks provide. Use them for quick trend identification on higher timeframes.

Understanding Timeframes

Each candle represents a specific time period. Common timeframes in binary options:

  • M1 (1 minute) — Each candle = 1 minute. Used for entries in short-term trading.
  • M5 (5 minutes) — Each candle = 5 minutes. Used for trend identification.
  • M15 (15 minutes) — Each candle = 15 minutes. Used for bigger picture context.
  • H1 (1 hour) — Each candle = 1 hour. Used for major support/resistance levels.
💡
Multi-Timeframe Approach: Check the trend on M5 or M15 first, then drop down to M1 for your entry. This gives you the best of both worlds — trend direction from the bigger picture and precise entries from the smaller timeframe.

Reading Support & Resistance

Support is a price level where buyers consistently step in and push the price back up. Think of it as a floor. Resistance is where sellers consistently push the price back down. Think of it as a ceiling.

To find these levels:

  1. Look for areas where price has bounced at least 2-3 times
  2. Draw a horizontal line at those price levels
  3. The more times price bounces off a level, the stronger that level is

Trading rule: CALL at support, PUT at resistance. When support breaks, it becomes new resistance (and vice versa).

Identifying Trends

  • Uptrend: Series of higher highs and higher lows. Each swing up goes higher than the previous one, and each pullback stays above the previous low.
  • Downtrend: Series of lower highs and lower lows. Each swing down goes lower, and each bounce stays below the previous high.
  • Sideways/Range: Price bounces between two horizontal levels with no clear direction. Draw a box around the range and trade bounces off the edges.

Drawing Trend Lines

A trend line connects at least two swing lows (in an uptrend) or two swing highs (in a downtrend). The more points it touches, the stronger the trend line. When price touches a trend line, it often bounces — this is your entry opportunity.

Putting It All Together

  1. Open your chart and set it to candlesticks
  2. Check M5 or M15 to identify the overall trend
  3. Mark key support and resistance levels
  4. Switch to M1 for entries
  5. Wait for price to reach a S/R level with a confirming candlestick pattern
  6. Enter your trade in the direction of the main trend
