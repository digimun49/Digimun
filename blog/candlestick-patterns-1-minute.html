Candlestick Patterns for 1-Minute Trading: Visual Guide

March 8, 202610 min readBy Digimun Pro Team

Candlestick patterns are the language of the market. Learn to read them, and you'll know what the market is about to do before it does it. This guide covers the 12 most reliable candlestick patterns specifically for 1-minute (M1) binary options trading.

Reversal Patterns (Bullish - CALL Signals)

1. Hammer

A candle with a small body at the top and a long lower wick (at least 2x the body length). The long wick shows that sellers pushed the price down, but buyers fought back and closed near the top. Signals a potential reversal from a downtrend to an uptrend.

How to trade: Look for a hammer at the bottom of a downtrend or at a support level. Enter CALL on the next candle open.

2. Bullish Engulfing

A large green candle that completely covers (engulfs) the previous red candle. This shows that buyers have overwhelmed sellers with strong momentum. The bigger the engulfing candle, the stronger the signal.

How to trade: Enter CALL when a bullish engulfing appears after 2-3 red candles at a support level.

3. Morning Star

A three-candle pattern: large red candle → small indecision candle (doji or spinning top) → large green candle. This is one of the most reliable reversal patterns and signals a shift from bearish to bullish sentiment.

How to trade: Enter CALL at the open of the candle following the large green candle.

4. Dragonfly Doji

A doji candle with a long lower shadow and virtually no upper shadow. The open and close are at or near the high of the candle. Shows strong rejection of lower prices — bulls are in control.

How to trade: CALL signal when found at support or after a downtrend. Stronger when volume is high.

Reversal Patterns (Bearish - PUT Signals)

5. Shooting Star

The opposite of a hammer — small body at the bottom with a long upper wick. Shows that buyers pushed the price up, but sellers rejected it and pushed it back down. Signals a potential reversal from uptrend to downtrend.

How to trade: Look for a shooting star at the top of an uptrend or at resistance. Enter PUT on the next candle.

6. Bearish Engulfing

A large red candle that completely covers the previous green candle. Indicates sellers have overwhelmed buyers. Very reliable at resistance levels or after extended uptrends.

How to trade: Enter PUT when a bearish engulfing appears after 2-3 green candles at resistance.

7. Evening Star

The bearish version of the morning star: large green candle → small indecision candle → large red candle. Signals the end of an uptrend and the beginning of a move down.

How to trade: Enter PUT at the open following the large red candle.

8. Gravestone Doji

A doji with a long upper shadow and virtually no lower shadow. Open and close are at or near the low. Shows complete rejection of higher prices — a strong bearish signal at resistance.

Indecision & Continuation Patterns

9. Doji (Standard)

Open and close are nearly identical, creating a cross or plus sign shape. Signals indecision — the market is pausing. By itself, a doji isn't a signal. You need to see what happens next. A doji at support followed by a green candle = CALL. A doji at resistance followed by a red candle = PUT.

10. Three White Soldiers

Three consecutive green candles, each closing higher than the previous. Signals strong bullish momentum. In M1 trading, this pattern usually continues for at least 1-2 more candles.

How to trade: Enter CALL on the fourth candle, but only if the third candle isn't unusually large (exhaustion).

11. Three Black Crows

Three consecutive red candles, each closing lower than the previous. The bearish counterpart to three white soldiers. Signals strong selling pressure.

How to trade: Enter PUT on the fourth candle.

12. Inside Bar

A candle whose entire range (high to low) fits within the previous candle's range. Shows consolidation before a move. The breakout direction of the next candle tells you which way to trade.

How to trade: Wait for the candle after the inside bar. If it breaks above the inside bar's high → CALL. Below the low → PUT.

🎯
M1 Trading Rule: On the 1-minute timeframe, candlestick patterns are most reliable when they form at clear support/resistance levels. A hammer in the middle of nowhere means much less than a hammer right at a key support level.

Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

  • CALL signals: Hammer, Bullish Engulfing, Morning Star, Dragonfly Doji, Three White Soldiers
  • PUT signals: Shooting Star, Bearish Engulfing, Evening Star, Gravestone Doji, Three Black Crows
  • Wait & confirm: Standard Doji, Inside Bar

Tips for Using Patterns on M1

  • Always check the trend on a higher timeframe (M5) before trading patterns on M1.
  • Patterns at support/resistance are 3x more reliable than patterns in empty space.
  • Don't trade every pattern — wait for the cleanest, most obvious ones.
  • Combine patterns with AI analysis for the highest probability trades.
  • Practice identifying patterns on a demo account before trading live.
