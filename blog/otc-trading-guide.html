OTC Trading Explained: How to Trade Binary Options 24/7

March 10, 20265 min readBy Digimun Pro Team

Ever wondered how you can trade binary options on weekends and holidays? That's where OTC (Over-The-Counter) markets come in. OTC trading gives you 24/7 access to binary options — no market closures, no waiting for Monday. Here's everything you need to know.

What is OTC Trading?

OTC stands for Over-The-Counter. Unlike regular (live) markets that operate during specific hours (London session, New York session, etc.), OTC markets are operated directly by the broker. This means they run 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year.

On platforms like Quotex and IQ Option, OTC pairs are usually labeled with an "(OTC)" tag — for example, EUR/USD (OTC), GBP/JPY (OTC), etc.

How OTC Markets Differ from Live Markets

  • Availability: OTC runs 24/7 including weekends and holidays. Live markets follow traditional exchange hours.
  • Price source: OTC prices are generated by the broker's internal systems, while live market prices come from global exchanges.
  • Volatility: OTC markets typically have lower and more predictable volatility compared to live markets.
  • Payouts: OTC payouts are sometimes slightly different (usually similar or slightly lower) than live market payouts.
  • Technical analysis: Traditional technical analysis works on OTC markets, but patterns may behave slightly differently.

Best Strategies for OTC Trading

OTC markets have their own personality. Here are strategies that work well:

1. Support & Resistance Bouncing

OTC markets tend to respect support and resistance levels strongly. Mark key horizontal levels and trade bounces off them. This is probably the most reliable OTC strategy.

2. Range Trading

OTC markets often move in defined ranges, especially during traditionally quiet hours. Identify the range boundaries and trade CALL at the bottom and PUT at the top.

3. Trend Following During Weekday OTC

During weekday OTC sessions (when live markets are also open), OTC pairs often mirror the trends of their live counterparts. Use the live market trend as a guide for OTC entries.

⚡
Weekend OTC Tip: Weekend OTC markets tend to be calmer with smaller candles. Reduce your profit expectations and trade smaller timeframes. Don't expect the same volatility you see on weekdays.

OTC Trading Tips

  • Start small: Trade with minimum amounts until you understand how OTC pairs move on your specific platform.
  • Focus on popular OTC pairs: EUR/USD OTC and GBP/USD OTC typically have the most predictable behavior.
  • Avoid choppy periods: If the OTC market is moving erratically with no clear direction, step away.

Is OTC Trading Right for You?

OTC trading is ideal if:

  • You can only trade on weekends or outside market hours
  • You prefer calmer, more predictable price action
  • You want to practice strategies without the chaos of live market volatility

However, if you're looking for strong trends and high-volatility setups, live markets during the London-New York overlap will serve you better.
